Measured Outcomes From Real Sessions

This is session data translated into plain language — what changed, what it means, and why it matters.

Immediate output shift

Strength that wasn’t showing up… showed up

During one session, several key muscles increased 30–40% on immediate retest. Hip flexor output improved from 28.6 → 43.6 lbs (left) and 29.3 → 40.7 lbs (right). Hamstrings and quads also increased — without changing the training program.

What that means: Strength that wasn’t contributing started contributing. Instead of one side doing too much work, force was distributed more evenly. The work you’re already doing began to translate better.

Consistency

Strength that finally shows up

Baseline output tested at 105.8 lbs. After identifying the strongest internal pairing, output increased to 129.4 lbs in the same session. No added weight. No added reps.

What that means: The strength was already there. RESET improved coordination so output became stronger and more consistent — not random.

Stability

Stability that holds under movement

Testing revealed weaker stabilization positions compared to baseline (88–90 lbs vs 102.9 lbs). Those weak links were trained directly and integrated into movement. After repeated sessions, there were no knee flare-ups while maintaining force output.

What that means: The unstable pattern was identified and reinforced. Instead of repeatedly stressing the same weak link, movement became more controlled under load.
